About me

My name is Erika and I welcome you to my project page.

So I decided to put it together, because I am excited about projects and task that have fallen in my path and will come my way in the future. I know there will be many interesting ones, so I want to look down the memory lane some day and remember them all and how they helped me grown.

My education path is somewhat out of the ordinary for an engineer. I actually started out as a student in a beauty school, training to be a hairdresser. However, as I never really wanted to become a hairdresser, I knew that my place was not in hairdressing salons. I did finish that beauty school with honors, but I never went on to practice the trade, as I did not feel “at home” with it. Some years did pass by and during those years, I was trying to find my “mission”. I tried working within many professions from sales to accounting (my mum was an accountant, so I thought it would make sense) and even drug rehabilitation centre as a person who had not had any drug related issues, thus I could help users to reintegrate into normal life.

All of the people that I have been working with, thought me among many other things, that it is important to know yourself, find what you like to do and go for it, no matter what. Therefore, I did! However, before I would even think about enrolling at any university, I had to have much better foundation of knowledge than what beauty school gave me. Therefore, I did finish not one, but two more educational programs (sales and economics) in preparation for university even if back then I still did not exactly know what my studies would be. However, in the middle of first program I realized that computer science is my passion, even if back then I did not even know exactly what it is. I enrolled in the faculty of computer science in Ljubljana and graduated with honors, despite of everyone but my mum (she had thought me to take on “the impossible”) was trying to convince me that I would have never been able to make it. I guess they were trying to protect me, best way they knew how, so I never took it as a bad thing.

From childhood on I was always sitting behind computer (whenever I could or my mum did not need it for her work) and fiddle with it for hours on end, but as a young girl I had never thought about working with computers as a profession, since it was considered a male domain. Looking back now I don't know how I or anyone else didn't see this possibility.

Now I love what I do and furthermore I am very excited about what lays ahead of me. Currently I work at a start-up, which is developing a cloud brokerage platform for a big telecom company and inspires to expand to foreign markets. As a s a software engineer I am blessed to work with amazingly smart people, who are sharing their gained knowledge and years of experience with me every day. We are working with Python programming language and are using Django as a web framework. Since my background was Java I was excited to learn new language, just to realize that it is actually not only super efficient but super fun as well. I did meet some of the community at my first Python conference at EuroPython 2012 in Florence, which inspired me to know more. I also regularly go to Django or Python meet ups in my town, but as we are a tiny country even community is tiny, but pleasant nevertheless.

My projects

bizstore.si

  • Some of the implementing technologies:

    Web application runs on AWS EC2 instance and is deployed with fabric library into development, integration and production environment. It is built within Django 1.3 framework. Data migrations are managed with third party application south, which is performing schema changes on DB. Python three engine mptt, was used to provide hierarchy to the services pages. For improving performance, we used django_compressor and many more

  • Overview:

    It's built according to the requirements of a telecom company, which had recently entered cloud brokerage market. The goal is to provide SOHO and SMEs with commodity like cloud services. Application offers a cloud based ordering and managing of those services, thus providing easy access with online product like shopping cart. At the back end is made as a custom CMS, to provide easy managing of the front end. Templates are selected by the system according to the url that was requested. If template with the url's name exists than it is displayed otherwise generic template is displayed. With this feature, we enabled designers to work with easy to use templating system.

  • Features:

    1. Social login as well as custom login forms. User profile is connected to Django's user.auth module and which is connected to the cloud services gateway. Social login is implemented with the third party component (janrain).
    2. Cloud based services are listed in special categories, for easy access
    3. Price list is automatically generated from range of available services, that are stored in gateway DB. Services also include features that come in bundle with the service and the kind that are orderable.
    4. Shopping cart includes order procedure which contains four steps. For better user experience we included jQuery into process for the purpose of calculating prices on the spot.
    5. Revision of ordered services by clients was implemented in order to provide them with easy to use managing of number of subscriptions for particular service.
    6. Customer is also provided with messaging system, to enable communication between technical stuff and themselves from application directly, but at the same time connecting to telecoms CRM.
  • My role:

    I was involved in the project from the start, as a developer and sometimes front end expert (HTML5, CSS3, Javascript). From implementing logic which presented shopping cart and processes that supported messaging system and many others, my task was also to keep the design responsive, which was also important since we provide access from anywhere, with any device.

URL: bizstore.si

POPP - customized CRM

  • Implementing technologies:

    Python – Django, AWS EC2, SES, fabric, south, lettuce, uni_form, mptt. Google Charts, pisa HTML to PDF conversion

  • Overview:

    Customized web based CRM, used by cloud Brokerage Company’s staff with intention of providing full support to sales and technicians communication. Applications priority functionality is to take sales staff trough the process of building tailor made quotes and subscribing customer to commodity cloud based services, from beginning (first visit with customer), to end when it also provides them with legal documents, that need to be signed within the companies policy. Applications neat feature is reporting, which is some custom SQL queries presented with Google charts javascript library. Sending the quote directly in to customers inbox is another feature achived with using AWS boto library for Python.

  • Features:

    1. Quotes generated in PDF format can be sent directly to clients mail from within the application. Email is seemingly sent from sales persons address, achieved with AWS boto library
    2. Easy access price list automatically generated from products portfolio in DB
    3. Dynamic services comparison calculator, built with MVVM Knockoutjs framework for javascript
    4. Domain availability lookup, for quick feedback to the customer about desired domain names
    5. Promotional material is stored in AWS S3 bucket, available for downloading at any time.
    6. Message service system is connected to technical staff as well as intended to tracking customers interaction with sales and technical staff

  • My role:

    My responsibility within this project was to implement features like dynamic UI for the domain availability, back-end service to check availability, building price comparison tool, generation of price list directly from db and implementing the entire messaging system. I was also taking care of the front-end experience and some of the design.

Unfortunately this is an internal company application and not available to the general public, so I can not provide an url.

Sales app

  • Technologies:

    Django 1.3, Javascript, Jquery, HTML5, CSS3, LESS

  • Overview:

    This application was made when in order for the company, which just entered the cloud brokerage market, to enable staff to educate them selfs about the services they were now selling. Since they just entered the marked and had previously no experiences with selling cloud based services, they are able to follow a series of questions and get the answers that were imperative in order to figure out best possible solutions for them. This tool is simply taking user step by step through questions that were defined earlier by the providers of the services.

  • My role:

    I undertook this project at the beginning of my employment with the company that is providing gateway for cloud services for the telecom companies. Project had few back end services that were written within Django framework, such as serving static pages, assembling data and pushing it to views. Everything else was made as a dynamic client side web application, built with jquery framework for javascript.

Unfortunately this is an internal company application and as such not available to the general public, so I can not provide url.
